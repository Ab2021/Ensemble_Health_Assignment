## 1. What Errors Would Matter More to the Business

A **false negative** -- the model fails to flag a claim that is actually denied -- is the more costly error. When a preventable denial leaves the queue unreviewed, the hospital incurs $25-$118 in rework, delayed payment, and potential permanent revenue loss (35-65% of denied claims are never resubmitted).

A **false positive** wastes a scarce review slot on a claim that would pay normally. At a ~21.6% denial rate, a random 25% review queue captures ~26% of denials. Our model captures **45.7%** -- nearly **double the random baseline** -- while only reviewing 25% of volume.

**Why recall is the wrong standalone metric:** Maximizing total recall without the 25% cap would flag far more claims than the team can review, rendering the system operationally useless. The right metric is **Denial Capture @ Top 25%** -- the percentage of true denials that land in the review queue.

| Metric | What It Measures | Value |
|---|---|---|
| Denial Capture @ 25% | How many denials are caught in the reviewable quarter | 45.7% |
| Precision @ 25% | Queue yield -- what fraction of reviewed claims are actually denied | 47.8% |
| ROC-AUC | Overall ranking quality | 0.692 |
| Brier Score | Probability calibration quality | 0.209 (0.137 calibrated) |

**Confusion matrix at frozen threshold (test):**
| | Predicted Paid | Predicted Denied |
|---|---|---|
| Actual Paid | 328 | 71 |
| Actual Denied | 76 | 64 |

## 2. Tell-Tale Data Findings for a Non-Technical Manager

### Finding 1: Medicaid MCO claims and Inpatient visits are the highest-risk segments

- **Medicaid MCO:** 30.6% denial rate (2x Commercial at 14.9%)
- **Inpatient:** 30.6% denial rate (vs. 18-20% for other visit types)
- Routing these segments to review first within the 25% queue is an easy operational win.

### Finding 2: Pre-submission gaps are strongly predictive and directly actionable

| Gap Present | Denial Rate |
|---|---|
| Prior auth required but missing | 46.5% |
| Missing documentation | 38.6% |
| Eligibility not verified | 36.0% |
| Referral required but missing | 33.0% |
| Compound: auth gap + missing docs | **74.0%** |

These are *fixable* before submission. The model identifies exactly these patterns -- not mysterious statistical signals, but concrete operational conditions the review team can act on.

### Finding 3: Denial rates are rising over time

- Train/validation denial rate: ~20%
- **Test (Nov-Dec 2024): 26.0%**

This temporal increase is realistic -- payer rules tighten, seasonal volume changes, and new policies shift. The model, trained on earlier months, must be monitored and periodically retrained.

## 3. What We Built and Why

### Model Selection: Logistic Regression (with XGB, RF comparisons)

| Model | Validation Capture@25% | Test AUC | Test Capture@25% |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dummy (stratified) | 27.2% | -- | -- |
| Random Forest | 45.6% | 0.678 | 44.3% |
| XGBoost | 42.7% | 0.642 | 41.4% |
| **Logistic Regression** | **49.5%** | **0.692** | **45.7%** |

**Why LR wins on this data:** The synthetic data is generated by simple additive rules (auth gap adds risk, missing docs adds separate risk, etc.). This is exactly the data-generating process LR is mathematically designed to model. Tree models overfit to spurious high-order interactions on small structured data (2,122 training rows).

**Implication for production:** An interpretable linear model with documented coefficients is also more auditable in a regulated billing environment than a black-box ensemble.

### Feature Engineering (pre-submission safe)

- **Gap features:** `auth_gap`, `referral_gap`, `total_admin_gaps`
- **Financial:** `payment_ratio`, `log_total_billed`, `billed_per_procedure`
- **Temporal:** `late_filing` (>30 days), `submission_delay`
- **Interactions:** `oon_auth_gap`, `double_deficit`, `is_high_risk_combo`

All derived from information available before submission. No post-outcome data used.

### Probability & Threshold Strategy

- Predicted probabilities are labeled `denial_probability` in the output CSV.
- **Frozen threshold:** 75th percentile of test-set probabilities, set once before any current claims are scored. This aligns the binary `predicted_denial` column to the operational 25% capacity.
- **Risk tiers:** High (top 25%), Medium (next 25%), Low (bottom 50%) -- rank-based, not threshold-based. These two concepts are kept distinct in the output.
- Calibration checked but found adequate (Brier=0.209); no need for additional Platt scaling.

### Leakage Prevention

- `is_denied` (target), `denial_reason` (post-outcome only), `split` (routing), and `claim_id` (identifier) are **programmatically excluded** from all model inputs.
- If any leakage column enters the feature matrix, the `validate_data.py` module fails loudly.

## 4. LLM Help Identify Claim-Specific Reasons for Potential Denials

### LLM Role Boundary

- **Does:** Translate model-derived risk factors into plain-English analyst notes
- **Does NOT:** Calculate risk, select claims, infer payer policies, generate clinical conclusions

The risk factors are computed by **LR coefficient x feature value** -- deterministic, mathematically traceable. The GenAI (or offline fallback template) converts these into readable 2-3 sentence explanations.

### Prompt Strategy

Versioned prompt in `prompts/explanation_prompt_v1.txt`:
- Structured JSON payload -- only factual fields + model-derived risk factors
- Mandatory uncertainty qualifier: "statistical estimate, not a guaranteed denial"
- Mandatory single actionable recommendation mapped to each risk factor
- No fabricated codes, dollar amounts, or clinical facts

### Example: Top-Risk Claim

> **Claim ID:** CCLM-00372 | **Denial Probability:** 94% | **Tier:** High
> **Factors:** Missing Required Prior Authorization, Compound deficit (missing auth + documentation), High-risk segment: Payer ID: P008
> **Explanation:** "This claim has an estimated denial risk of 94% -- this is a statistical estimate, not a guaranteed outcome. A key concern is that missing required prior authorization. Recommended action: Confirm whether the required prior authorization can be obtained and documented."

### Example: Low-Risk Claim (Behavior Test)

> **Claim ID:** CCLM-00442 | **Denial Probability:** 11% | **Tier:** Low
> **Explanation:** "This claim has an estimated denial risk of 11% -- this is a statistical estimate, not a guaranteed outcome. No actionable pre-submission risk flags are evident based on available data. Routine submission with standard verification is recommended."

**Validation:** The claim correctly does NOT invent risks, does NOT force an action, and acknowledges the low risk.

### Safety Guards

| Guard | Implementation |
|---|---|
| Temperature=0 | Factual consistency |
| Structured JSON input | No raw claim text, no denial reasons |
| Field-grounded mapping | Every sentence traces to actual model features |
| Output validation | Automated checks: uncertainty, one action, no hallucination |
| Manual QA | Inspected all 10 high-risk outputs |
| API key hygiene | Read from env; never committed |
| Fallback template | Works with zero API access -- deterministic, reproducible |

## 5. Active Learning Experiment Results

We executed **5 independent model experiments** using the `src/experiment_tracker.py` infrastructure to find the optimal production architecture.

### 5.1 Experiment Design

| Exp # | Experiment | Hypothesis | Architecture |
|:---:|:---|:---|:---|
| 1 | LR Baseline | Linear additive rules dominate | LogisticRegression(C=0.1, balanced) |
| 2 | LR Calibrated | Probability calibration improves tier reliability | CalibratedClassifierCV(LR, cv=5, sigmoid) |
| 3 | XGBoost Tuned | Non-linear interactions capture hidden patterns | XGBClassifier(max_depth=6, lr=0.05, scale_pos_weight=3.6) |
| 4 | Random Forest Robust | Ensemble averaging reduces variance | RandomForestClassifier(n_est=200, max_depth=12, balanced) |
| 5 | LR + Interactions | Multiplicative features boost high-risk capture | LR + billed_x_admin_gaps, billed_x_doc_issue |

### 5.2 Results Leaderboard

| Rank | Experiment | Validation Capture@25% | ROC-AUC | Brier | Status |
|:---:|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---|
| **T1** | **LR Calibrated** | **49.51%** | **0.710** | **0.137** | PRODUCTION |
| T1 | LR Baseline | 49.51% | 0.711 | 0.209 | Baseline |
| T1 | LR + Interactions | 49.51% | 0.707 | 0.210 | No gain |
| 4 | Random Forest | 39.81% | 0.653 | 0.155 | Underperforms |
| 5 | XGBoost | 35.92% | 0.615 | 0.180 | Severe underperformance |

### 5.3 Key Findings

1. **All three LR variants tie on Capture@25%** (49.51%) -- confirming that the data is fundamentally linear-additive. Adding interaction terms or calibration does not change the ranking, which is what matters for the review queue.

2. **Calibration delivers the decisive win:** Brier score drops from 0.209 to 0.137 -- a **34% improvement** in probability reliability. This means the High/Medium/Low tier boundaries are far more trustworthy for operations staff.

3. **Tree models fail on this synthetic data** -- both XGBoost (35.9%) and RF (39.8%) underperform LR by 10+ percentage points. They overfit to spurious interactions from the 53 post-preprocessing features on a small training set (2,122 rows).

4. **Interaction features add no value** -- billed_x_admin_gaps and billed_x_doc_issue do not improve the signal. The synthetic data does not have multiplicative effects between financial amount and gap flags.

### 5.4 Production Recommendation

**Deploy Calibrated Logistic Regression (Experiment 2)** as the production model because:
- Same denial capture as uncalibrated LR (best in class)
- Dramatically better probability calibration (Brier 0.137 vs 0.209)
- Simpler audit trail than tree ensembles
- Faster inference (no ensemble overhead)
- Coefficients are directly interpretable for compliance review

### 6.3 Quality Checks Performed

For every API response, the auditor validates:
1. **Uncertainty disclaimer** -- must contain "estimate", "statistical", or "not guaranteed"
2. **Actionable recommendation** -- must suggest "verify", "obtain", "review", "attach", etc.
3. **Minimum length** -- response must be >= 50 characters
4. **Hallucination markers** -- flags potential PII (SSN, DOB, patient name) or clinical codes (ICD-, CPT-)

### 6.4 Why This Matters

- **Token tracking** enables cost estimation when scaling to thousands of claims
- **Latency logging** identifies API degradation before it affects operations
- **Quality validation** catches bad LLM outputs before they reach analysts
- **Fallback logging** distinguishes API failures from low-risk bypasses
- **JSON audit logs** are HIPAA-safe -- no data leaves the machine; stored as structured JSON

## 7. Limitations and Improvements with More Time

1. **Multi-class denial reason prediction** -- instead of just deny/not-deny, predict the likely denial reason (missing docs, eligibility, etc.) to give the analyst a specific correction target.
2. **Dollar-weighted optimization** -- $50,000 inpatient denials hurt more than $500 outpatient denials. Maximize expected dollar capture, not just claim count.
3. **Temporal cross-validation** -- train on rolling windows (Jan-Mar, Feb-Apr, ...) to better simulate production where claims are scored chronologically.
4. **Payer-specific models** -- separate models per payer capture payer-specific rules; ensemble with the global model.
5. **Real LLM API integration** -- replace the deterministic template with GPT-4o or Gemini to generate more natural, varied language while maintaining grounding.
6. **Continuous monitoring** -- track feature drift (PSI), denial rate shifts, and model degradation; trigger retraining when drift exceeds thresholds.
7. **Clinical NLP on denial reasons** -- cluster `denial_reason` text to discover emerging patterns that structured features miss.
8. **Cost-optimal tiering** -- optimize the 125/125/250 split based on marginal value of additional reviews, not just equal percentile buckets.
