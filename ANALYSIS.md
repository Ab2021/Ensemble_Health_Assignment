# Ensemble Health -- Detailed Analysis: Claim Denial Risk Prediction

## 1. Executive Summary

This document provides a complete technical analysis of the pre-bill denial prediction system built for Ensemble Health Partners. The system ingests 3,200 historical claims and 500 current claims, engineers 53 pre-submission features, trains and evaluates 10 model variants, and deploys a calibrated Logistic Regression model to production. Top-10 high-risk claims receive plain-English explanations generated by Ollama Cloud (gemma4:31b-cloud) via structured JSON prompts validated through Pydantic.

**Production recommendation:** Calibrated Logistic Regression (sigmoid, cv=5) -- delivers 45.7% denial capture within the top 25% review window and a Brier score of 0.137, representing a 34% improvement in probability calibration over the uncalibrated baseline (Brier 0.209).

## 2. Model Selection & Experiment Results

### 2.1 Experiment Design

Ten model variants were evaluated on a fixed 70/15/15 train/validation/test split. The primary metric is **denial capture at top 25%** -- the fraction of all true denials that fall within the highest-risk quartile, representing the claims that would be flagged for manual review under a 25% capacity constraint.

| Rank | Experiment | Type | Capture@25 | ROC-AUC | Brier |
|:---:|:---|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 1 | exp1_lr_baseline | LR, C=0.1, balanced | 49.51% | 0.711 | 0.209 |
| 1 | exp2_lr_calibrated | LR + Platt scaling | 49.51% | 0.710 | 0.137 |
| 1 | exp5_lr_interactions | LR + interaction features | 49.51% | 0.707 | 0.210 |
| 4 | exp6_gradient_boosting | GBM, depth=5, lr=0.05 | 48.54% | 0.708 | 0.165 |
| 5 | exp7_voting_ensemble | Soft Voting (LR+RF+GBM) | 48.54% | 0.705 | 0.172 |
| 6 | exp8_stacking | Stacking, LR meta, cv=5 | 46.60% | 0.698 | 0.181 |
| 7 | exp10_svc_rbf | SVC RBF + Platt scaling | 45.63% | 0.688 | 0.192 |
| 8 | exp9_mlp_neural | MLP (64,32), ReLU | 43.69% | 0.672 | 0.198 |
| 9 | exp4_rf_robust | RF, depth=12, balanced | 39.81% | 0.653 | 0.155 |
| 10 | exp3_xgb_tuned | XGB, depth=6, lr=0.05 | 35.92% | 0.615 | 0.180 |

### 2.2 Key Findings

**Strong linear signal dominates.** The dataset's most predictive features are binary administrative flags: `auth_gap` (missing prior authorization), `doc_issue` (missing documentation flag), `referral_gap` (missing referral), and `network_issue` (out-of-network provider). These directly encode whether a claim is missing a required administrative step before submission. Linear models naturally learn these additive relationships.

**Non-linear models underperform.** XGBoost (35.92%), Random Forest (39.81%), MLP (43.69%), and SVC (45.63%) all score below LR. The reason is that tree-based models split on features independently, diluting the additive signal. Neural networks require more data than 3,200 rows to learn meaningful non-linear interactions.

**Gradient Boosting narrowly trails LR.** At 48.54%, GBM is the best non-linear alternative, only 0.97 percentage points below LR. Its sequential boosting with shallow trees (depth=5) better captures additive patterns than XGBoost's deeper trees.

**Ensemble methods don't beat LR.** Voting (48.54%) and Stacking (46.60%) combine LR with weaker tree models, diluting the strong LR signal. The meta-learner in Stacking fails to find a better combination than pure LR.

### 2.3 Production Model: Calibrated Logistic Regression

Selected for three reasons:

1. **Equal capture, better calibration.** Calibrated LR matches the baseline's 49.51% validation capture@25 while reducing the Brier score from 0.209 to 0.137 (34% improvement). This means the High/Medium/Low tier assignments are substantially more reliable.

2. **Interpretability.** LR coefficients map directly to business-relevant risk factors (missing auth, missing referral, late filing). This supports the GenAI explanation engine downstream.

3. **Simplicity.** A calibrated linear model is easier to deploy, monitor, and explain to stakeholders than an ensemble or neural network.

**Production configuration:**
- `CalibratedClassifierCV(LogisticRegression(C=0.1, class_weight='balanced', max_iter=1000), cv=5, method='sigmoid')`
- Retrained on full history (train + val + test = 3,200 claims)
- Threshold: 75th percentile of validation probabilities (0.252)

## 4. Feature Engineering

### 4.1 Base Features (from raw data)
- `total_billed`, `expected_payment`, `num_procedures`, `num_diagnoses`, `days_to_submit`
- `prior_auth_required`, `has_prior_auth`, `is_in_network`, `missing_documentation_flag`
- `eligibility_verified`, `referral_required`, `referral_present`
- `payer_type`, `visit_type`, `payer_id`, `service_month`

### 4.2 Engineered Features
- **Gap flags:** `auth_gap`, `referral_gap`, `doc_issue`, `elig_issue`, `network_issue`
- **Composite scores:** `total_admin_gaps` (sum of all gaps), `complexity_score` (procedures x diagnoses)
- **Financial ratios:** `payment_ratio`, `payment_gap`, `billed_per_procedure`, `billed_per_diagnosis`
- **Log transforms:** `log_total_billed`, `log_expected_payment`
- **Temporal:** `service_month_num`, `service_quarter`, `submission_delay` (binned), `late_filing`
- **Interactions:** `oon_auth_gap`, `oon_referral_gap`, `double_deficit`, `is_high_risk_combo`
- **Risk segment:** High-risk payer + visit type combination (Medicaid MCO + Inpatient/Emergency)

### 4.3 Leakage Prevention
The following columns are programmatically excluded before model ingestion:
- `claim_id` -- identifier
- `split` -- train/val/test label
- `is_denied` -- target variable
- `denial_reason` -- post-submission information
- `service_month`, `service_month_dt` -- temporal identifiers

All categorical features (`payer_type`, `visit_type`, `payer_id`) are one-hot encoded with `handle_unknown='ignore'` to handle unseen categories in current claims.

## 5. Explaining Denial Risk

### 5.1 Risk Factor Extraction

Risk factors are derived from Logistic Regression coefficients. The process:
1. Fit a plain (non-calibrated) LR on the full preprocessed feature matrix
2. For each claim, compute `coefficient x feature_value` for every feature
3. Rank features by absolute contribution and select the top 3
4. Map raw feature names to human-readable labels (e.g., `auth_gap` -> "Missing Required Prior Authorization")
5. Identify compound risks: if both `auth_gap` and `doc_issue` are flagged, output "Compound deficit: missing auth + documentation"

### 5.2 GenAI Explanations (gemma4:31b-cloud)

Top 10 highest-risk claims receive LLM-generated explanations. The pipeline:

```
claim data + risk factors -> ExplanationRequest (Pydantic)
  -> structured prompt -> ollama.chat(gemma4:31b-cloud)
  -> JSON response -> ExplanationResponse (Pydantic)
  -> plain text explanation
```

**Quality checks (100% pass rate in production):**
- Disclaimer: Must contain uncertainty keywords ("estimate", "statistical", "not guaranteed")
- Actionable: Must suggest a corrective action (obtain, verify, resolve, attach, confirm)
- Min length: >50 characters
- Hallucination scan: Checks for ICD/CPT codes, patient names, SSN, DOB markers

**Fallback strategy:**
- Claims with prob < 0.25 or "No actionable" factors: deterministic template (no API call)
- API failures: regex extraction from raw response -> deterministic template

## 8. Experiment Tracker & Active Learning

### 8.1 Tracker Design

`src/experiment_tracker.py` is a self-contained, HIPAA-safe experiment manager with an MLflow-compatible artifact layout. Each run stores:
- `params.json` -- hyperparameters
- `metrics.json` -- evaluation metrics
- `model.pkl` -- serialized sklearn model
- `preprocessor.pkl` -- ColumnTransformer
- `predictions.csv` -- scored current claims
- `feature_names.json` -- ordered feature list

### 8.2 Active Learning Insights

Across 10 experiments spanning 5 model families:

1. **LR variants consistently hit 49.51% capture@25** -- this is the ceiling for the current dataset
2. **GBM (48.54%) is the best non-linear model** -- close to LR, suitable if regulatory requirements demand non-linear alternatives
3. **Calibration is the highest-value improvement** -- same capture, 34% better Brier, no downside
4. **Feature interactions add no value** -- the engineered gap flags already capture the relevant interactions
5. **Ensembles degrade LR** -- Voting/Stacking combine strong LR with weaker trees, diluting performance

### 8.3 Future Experiment Directions
- SMOTE/ADASYN for handling class imbalance differently
- Feature selection (RFE, SelectKBest) to reduce noise from 53 features
- LightGBM or CatBoost as additional gradient boosting alternatives
- Bayesian hyperparameter optimization (Optuna) for GBM/LR tuning
- Threshold optimization based on cost matrices (cost of false positive vs false negative)

## 9. Limitations & Recommendations

### 9.1 Known Limitations
- **Synthetic data:** Real claims include ICD-10/CPT codes, 835 remittance data, and clinical notes that would significantly improve prediction
- **Temporal window:** Current claims (2025-01) follow training data ending at 2024-12; distribution drift is possible
- **Single dataset:** 3,200 claims is sufficient for LR but small for deep learning
- **Binary outcome:** Multi-class denial reasons (medical necessity vs administrative vs coding) could enable more targeted interventions
- **No payer-specific models:** Different payers have different denial patterns; a single global model may miss segment-specific signals

### 9.2 Production Recommendations
1. **Deploy Calibrated LR** for its combination of high capture, reliable probabilities, and interpretability
2. **Retrain monthly** as new claims and denial outcomes become available
3. **Monitor Brier score** as an early warning for distribution drift
4. **A/B test the 25% review workflow** -- measure actual denial reduction vs control group
5. **Add ICD/CPT features** when real claims data becomes available
6. **Segment models by payer type** if per-payer data volumes are sufficient
